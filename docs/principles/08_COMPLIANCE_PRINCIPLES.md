# PXL ERP Compliance Principles

Version: PXL Constitution v1.0
Status: Canonical governing principles
Scope: Philippine tax, CAS readiness, compliance data capture, filing, generated documents, audit, and regulatory traceability

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent compliance principles for PXL ERP. Compliance is part of the accounting system foundation, not a later reporting feature.

## Core Compliance Principles

### 1. PH Compliance First

PXL ERP must support Philippine private-sector compliance requirements as a Phase 1 foundation concern.

### 2. Compliance Profile Drives Behavior

Company compliance behavior must come from compliance profiles, tax setup, registration data, filing setup, and effective-dated configuration. It must not be hardcoded per company in UI or backend code.

### 3. Snapshot Compliance Facts

Transactions must preserve compliance-sensitive details as of transaction time, including registered names, TINs, addresses, tax classifications, rates, ATC codes, document numbers, and withholding facts where required.

### 4. Effective Dates Are Mandatory

Tax rules, compliance registrations, rates, exemptions, filing obligations, and document formats must support effective dates or versioning where historical accuracy matters.

### 5. CAS Readiness Is Built In

Computerized Accounting System readiness requires traceability, audit logs, sequential document controls, generated document history, books, source-to-ledger links, and controlled correction workflows.

### 6. Compliance Outputs Are Traceable

SLSP, RELIEF, QAP, SAWT, VAT schedules, withholding certificates, books, income tax support, and generated BIR-facing outputs must trace to source transactions and retained snapshots.

### 7. Filed Compliance Records Are Protected

Filed, submitted, exported, approved, or locked compliance records must not be casually edited. Corrections require controlled amendment, reversal, regeneration, or re-filing workflows.

### 8. Compliance And Accounting Must Reconcile

Tax reports and compliance files must reconcile to accounting postings, source documents, tax detail tables, and ledger outputs.

### 9. Generated Compliance Files Need Metadata

Compliance files must store generation metadata, source period, company, file type, status, generated by, generated at, file location, hash where applicable, and traceability to source records.

### 10. Tax Rules Must Not Be UI-Only

The UI may guide users, but tax treatment must be enforced by database setup, backend services, posting rules, and compliance validation.

### 11. Import And Export Compliance Is Auditable

Compliance-related imports and exports must have job tracking, errors, source files, output files, user context, and review trail.

### 12. Master Data Compliance Fields Matter

Customer, supplier, company, branch, item, service, and tax master data must capture compliance fields needed for correct document generation, withholding, reporting, and audit.

### 13. Document Numbering Must Be Controlled

Official documents must follow configured numbering series, scope, status, and generated-document traceability rules.

### 14. Compliance Visibility Is Permissioned

Compliance pages, reports, generated files, and filing actions must be controlled by permissions, feature visibility, workspace assignment, and RLS.

### 15. Compliance Tests Are Required

Compliance workflows must have tests or verification scenarios covering tax calculation, snapshot behavior, filing status guards, generated outputs, amendment paths, and report reconciliation.
